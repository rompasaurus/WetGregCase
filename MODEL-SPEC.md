# WetGregCase — Model Specification

A two-piece **slim** 3D-printed enclosure for the **Dilder Full PCB Rev 1**,
generated by `freecad/wetgreg_case.FCMacro`. The macro imports the real KiCad
STEP assembly (aligned, with unpopulated header pins stripped and the screen
shifted to meet the front face) and adds a snap-on joystick thumbpiece.

> Status: **v2.** Screen + joystick on the front; thin USB-C-height backplate on
> the Pico side; aggressive curved front; ~16.3 mm thick.

---

## 1. Orientation & coordinate frame

`local = (KiCad_X − 46.70,  KiCad_StepY + 88.0,  Z)` — **Z = 0 is the PCB bottom
face**. The board occupies X 0..97.8, Y 0..42.012.

| Side | Z | Contents | Case part |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Front** | **−Z** | WeAct screen + 5-way joystick | **Front cover** (deep, curved, window) |
| **Back** | **+Z** | Pico W, USB-C, power switch | **Backplate** (thin, USB-C height) |

The screen and joystick are on the **same side** (−Z); the screen window and the
joystick hole/thumbpiece are therefore both on the front cover.

## 3. Architecture

```
            ┌──────────── aggressive front curve (r6) ────────────┐
  Z=-11.40  ╰─╮   screen window  +  joystick hole / thumbpiece   ╭─╯  front face = screen top
            FRONT COVER (screen side, deep) — curved outer edge
  Z= 0.00 ══╪════════════ FLUSH lap joint (PCB-bottom plane) ════╪══  sides flush
            BACKPLATE (pico side) — thin frame, open centre
            shelf (Z 1.6) the PCB rests on · USB-C (+X) · switch (−Y)
  Z=+4.90   ╰──────────────────────────────────────────────────╯  = USB-C height
```

### Front cover (screen side, −Z)
- Outer face **meets the screen top at Z = −11.4** (your standoff measurement);
  only a thin `front_floor` (1.2 mm) frame around the screen window.
- **Screen window** over the active area (real STEP location, 1.5 mm bezel).
- **Joystick hole** (Ø12) + **thumbpiece** on this side.
- **Aggressive curve** (r = 6 mm, auto-reduced if geometry needs) on the front
  outer perimeter edge so the case looks thinner than it is.
- Rises to the flush lap joint at Z = 0 with an interlocking tongue + snap nibs.

### Backplate (pico side, +Z) — thin
- Only as tall as the **USB-C connector** (outer face Z = +4.9). The USB-C marks
  where the base begins.
- A perimeter **frame with a small shelf** (at the PCB top, reaches 1.6 mm over
  the board edge) that the **PCB rests on**. **Open centre** so it never touches
  a component (Pico stays exposed/accessible).
- **USB-C opening** (+X) and **power-switch slot** (−Y) for external toggling.
- Flush outer footprint with the front cover; snap-detent recesses capture the
  front tongue's nibs.

### Thumbpiece
- Snap cap over the K1-1506SN-01 actuator (rev2-mk2 style): Ø11 disc, concave
  thumb dish, 3.3 mm square socket. On the front (screen) side.

meet the front face).

## 5. Open items / confirm during validation

1. **Screen height (11.4 mm).** Front face is built to this. The KiCad 3D model
   originally had the screen at 8.64 mm; the macro shifts it to 11.4 so it meets
   the front face — confirm your real standoffs give 11.4 mm.
2. **Pico height (5.26 mm) vs backplate (4.9 mm).** The Pico's tallest parts
   exceed the backplate top by ~0.36 mm; the open centre means it's exposed (no
   collision). If you want it enclosed, raise `base_above`.
3. **Backplate is an open frame** (won't touch components). If you'd rather have
   a closed back skin, say so (needs local pockets for the Pico/USB).
4. **Screen window = full module area − bezel.** If you want the smaller active
   glass area only, give me its offset and I'll tighten it.
5. **Switch slot / USB opening** sized to the STEP footprints; confirm actuator
   travel and plug clearance.
6. No bolt bosses (closure is snap + shelf); set `import_assembly=False` for a
   case-only run.

## 6. How to run

```bash
cd freecad && freecadcmd wetgreg_case.FCMacro     # headless regenerate
# or open the macro in the FreeCAD GUI → Execute
```

`PARAMS["explode_z"]` (e.g. 20) for an exploded view. Outputs:
`freecad/WetGregCase.FCStd` and STEP files for the front cover, backplate and
thumbpiece in `exports/`.
